Yiling Li

I am a fourth-year undergraduate student at Princeton University majoring in Electrical & Computer Engineering with minors in Statistics & Machine Learning, Robotics, and Visual Arts. I am applying to PhD programs in Electrical Engineering for Autumn 2026.

My research focuses on advancing light-driven technologies at the intersection of nanophotonics and computational imaging. I am particularly interested in how nanoscale structures, material systems, and algorithmic models can be co-designed to control light through photon upconversion, metasurface engineering, or holographic display architectures for next-generation optoelectronic devices and display systems.

I am currently working with Professor Felix Heide at Princeton Computational Imaging Lab for my senior thesis on the optimization of metasurface neural etendue expansion with hardware-in-the-loop.
